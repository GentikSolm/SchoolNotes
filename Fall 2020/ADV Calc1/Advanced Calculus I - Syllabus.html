EVALUATION:

There will be two midterm exams and a final exam that will be given online. The midterm exams are tentatively scheduled on 09/24/20 and 10/29/20. The final exam will be given during the regularly scheduled final exam period. On the day of an exam, I will post it on my webpage at 10:45 am. Simultaneously, you will have to connect via Webex with your camera on and the microphone off (unless you need to ask a question). You will have one hour and fifteen minutes to work on the exam and submit your answers back to me (possibly, more time will be given during the final). You will have to work it out on paper, scan, and send it back to me IN PDF FORMAT in order to get credit. It is essential that you show all of your work. The use of any external help during the exams, e.g., assitance from other students or from online services such as Chegg is not allowed.

GRADES:

The final grade will be based on the number of points (out of 450) that you will accumulate during the semester. In addition to midterms (100 points each) and the final (150 points), the total score takes into account homework, with the 100 points alloted for this purpose.

IMPORTANT: If your performance on the final is better than your performance on the other exams, the final might be counted more heavily than 150 points.

MAKE-UP WORK:

Make-up exams will be given and late homework will be accepted only in the case of a documented medical problem or ``emergency''. Permission to take a make-up exam should be obtained prior to the exam (if possible). Make-up exams will be given at the time determined by the professor.

HOMEWORK:

Homework will be posted on web page approximately every other Thursday. The due date for each assignment will be on Thursday, two weeks after the homework was posted. Problems marked with an asterisk will be collected and graded; the others may be discussed, but need not to be turned in. The homework should be submitted to me by emailing a pdf file to dmitry at uakron dot edu.

The importance of homework cannot be over-stressed: one can only learn mathematics by doing many exercises! Doing homework and writing up the solutions allows you to test yourself to see whether you really understand the material. This also protects you from being "surprised" on the exams.

REVIEW PROBLEMS:

Review problems will be distributed several days prior to each test to give you better understanding of the material. However the tests will not necessarily contain problems similar to the ones from the corresponding review sheet.
